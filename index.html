The Rise and Consequences of AI Image Generation

5 years ago, barely anyone cared about AI images or how to generate them because the technology simply didn't exist. DALL-E, the OpenAI online image generation platform launched to the public late in 2022, and we can see an immediate spike in interest across Google that has generally continued to grow. As the tools have become more well known, there has been a slight falloff in interest, but is clearly a topic that has exploded in recent years.

Although AI image generation is quite new, the rate at which it has occurred is staggering. An estimated 12 billion images have been generated each year since its inception in 2022. This number is almost certainly an undercount when taking into account image generation on local machines and the sparsity of available data on the subject. If this trend continues, by 2030 there will have been over 100 billion images generated by AI. For context, according to some estimations, Google Images contains a total number of somewhere between 75 - 150 billion images.

With the rise of AI generated content, large data centers for generating content followed closely behind. In the United States alone, there are currently over 1,200 different data centers for generating and training different AI, with more to come. Many of them are owned by single parent companies such as Microsoft, Amazon, and Apple. As time progresses, we can expect to see more and more data centers in use, especially for more and more training to make AI better and better.
